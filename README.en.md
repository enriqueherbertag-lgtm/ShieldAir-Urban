# ShieldAir-Urban: Active urban lungs against smog

In cities like New Delhi, Lahore, Beijing, or Santiago, air pollution reaches emergency levels. Smog affects the health of millions, collapses hospitals, and reduces life expectancy.

Traditional solutions (filters, home purifiers) are individual and do not address the problem at an urban scale.

**ShieldAir-Urban was created to change that.**

## What it is

ShieldAir-Urban is a 28-meter modular tower installed on the rooftops of strategic buildings (hospitals, government offices, hotels). It transforms those buildings into "active urban lungs": capturing smog at street level, recovering waste heat, and generating biomass, with no upfront cost to the municipality.

The system is self-funding: revenue from heat sales, carbon credits, and biomass covers operation and allows scaling.

## What it does

- **Captures smog**: sucks 28,000 m³ of air per hour, reducing PM2.5 within a 350-meter radius by up to 40%.
- **Recovers heat**: 78 kW net thermal (ΔT of 6°C) integrated into the anchor building's heating or hot water system, reducing its energy consumption by 40-60%.
- **Produces biomass**: 15 kg/day of algae (Scenedesmus dimorphus + Chlorella) converted into biofertilizer or protein.
- **Captures CO₂**: 1.1 tons per day (400 tons per year).

## Who it is for

- **Municipalities**: no upfront investment, pay for measurable air quality improvement results.
- **Anchor buildings**: 40-60% energy savings on heating/hot water.
- **Citizens**: reduced smog exposure in daily life.

## Main advantages

- **No upfront cost for the city**: municipality pays only for measurable results.
- **Self-funding**: revenue from heat, carbon credits, and biomass covers operation.
- **Uses existing infrastructure**: installed on rooftops of already-built buildings.
- **Scalable**: a single tower cleans a 350-meter radius. 50 towers can transform an entire district.

## Roadmap (18 months)

- Phase 0 (months 1-3): MoU with municipality, selection of pilot buildings, structured financing.
- Phase 1 (months 4-9): modular manufacturing and "plug and play" installation on rooftops.
- Phase 2 (months 10-12): continuous monitoring, impact reporting, pay-for-results activation.
- Phase 3 (months 13-18): metropolitan scaling (50 buildings).

## Scaled impact (50 towers in New Delhi)

- Clean air produced: 1.4 million m³/hour.
- Heat recovered: 3.3 MW thermal (enough for 1,000 homes).
- Biomass generated: 275 tons/year (protein for 2,500 people/day).
- CO₂ captured: 20,000 tons/year.
- PM2.5 reduction: up to 45%.
- Local jobs created: 180.
